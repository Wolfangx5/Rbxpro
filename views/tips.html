Choose Your Date of Birth Wisely

Some surveys may target a specific age group or demographic, so you need to be careful about the date of birth you enter. A good rule of thumb is to say that you are around 25 years old, which means you were born in 1996. Always keep it consistent across surveys. For example, use April 13, 1996. If asked about children, say they are 5 years old.

Choosing Your ZIP Code

Open Google and search for "Postal code." Choose a ZIP code and ensure it matches the code you use when completing surveys. Consistency is key!

Avoid Certain Industries

Always say NO if asked whether you or your family members work in industries like advertising, marketing, media, or research. Instead, select industries like IT Hardware or IT Services, which are preferred by survey providers.

Be Realistic About Your Income

When asked about your income, choose a realistic range. A good option is around $150,000, which is above average but not unrealistic.

State Your Education Level

Select Undergraduate as your education level. This makes you eligible for most surveys without appearing overqualified.

Select Your Employment Status

Choose Full-Time as your employment status. Additionally, select big roles like CEO and say you work in companies with 1,000-5,000 employees.

Claim Your Purchasing Decisions

Always state that you make all purchasing decisions in your household. This increases your value for surveys focused on consumer habits.

Cater to the Survey Topic

Adjust your answers to fit the survey topic. For instance, if a survey is about smartphones, claim that you own and use one regularly.

Watch Out for Test Questions

Some surveys include test questions to ensure you're paying attention. Read every question carefully to avoid disqualification.
